📘 User Guide & Key Species (click to expand)

This tool identifies Tasmanian vegetation communities based on structural and ecological traits.

  • Structure: Forest, woodland, scrub, treeless
  • Moisture: Dry, wet, waterlogged, coastal, alpine
  • Dominant: Vegetation group (eucalypt, rainforest, buttongrass, melaleuca, sedges)
  • Elevation: Low, mid, high
  • Species: Optional comma-separated observations

🌱 Key Species Reference

Forests: Eucalyptus obliqua, E. regnans, Nothofagus cunninghamii, Atherosperma moschatum

Woodlands: Eucalyptus viminalis, E. ovata, Banksia marginata

Scrub & Heath: Leptospermum scoparium, Melaleuca squarrosa, Epacris impressa

Wetlands: Gymnoschoenus sphaerocephalus, Baumea, Juncus, Carex, Sphagnum

Alpine: Richea scoparia, Podocarpus lawrencei
